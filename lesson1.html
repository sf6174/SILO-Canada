LESSON 1: BIODIVERSITY

Imagine you are trying to bake a cake. You gather all the ingredients: flour, eggs, baking soda, vanilla extract, butter, and sugar. But wait! You realize you are out of baking soda. What happens when there is a missing ingredient? Something isn’t right!

Just like every ingredient in a recipe is essential for baking a successful cake, every animal and organism is essential for maintaining a thriving ecosystem.

BUT WAIT – WHAT IS AN ECOSYSTEM EXACTLY?

Think of it like a community that exists in the natural world. It consists of both living and nonliving things all within a natural environment that enables these components to interact.

For example, in Zootopia every “town” is made up of a different ecosystem, providing the environment those species need to thrive in.

STORY

APPLE ORCHARD COMMUNITY

Imagine a small town. In this town, there’s a mailman who delivers letters, a grocer who provides food, and a doctor who ensures a healthy community. Each person has an important role; having diverse roles in a community is what keeps everything running smoothly. Now imagine if one person suddenly disappeared—say, the grocer. Food supplies run low, people fall ill, and the town becomes disorganized.

Now, think of an apple orchard as its own small town. The apple farmer is like the mayor, making sure everything runs smoothly. But just like the town needs the mailman, the grocer, and the doctor, the apple orchard also needs its diverse workforce community.

EXPLANATION

In Apple Town, every plant, animal, and microorganism plays a unique role. Each helper is essential for a functioning orchard community.

THE ROLES

BEES: The mailmen, pollinating apple blossoms so they can turn into apples.

• I visit flowers and spread pollen so they can turn into apples.
• Without me, apple trees wouldn’t produce fruit!

FUNGI: The town’s cleaners, breaking down dead leaves and branches to recycle them.

• I break down dead leaves and branches.
• I recycle nutrients back into the soil to keep the orchard healthy!

EARTHWORMS: The grocers, enriching the soil with nutrients for healthy tree growth.

• I dig through the soil, breaking it up and adding nutrients.
• I help apple trees grow strong and healthy!

WOODPECKERS: The doctors, treating trees by removing disease-causing insects.

• I peck at trees to remove insects that could make them sick.
• I help keep the trees strong and disease-free!

LIVESTOCK: The gardeners, controlling weeds around the orchard.

• I eat weeds and unwanted plants around the orchard.
• I help keep the land clear for apple trees to grow.
