ANALOGY: CANDY EXAMPLE

ACTIVITY: “BBOBBY THE BIOLOGIST AND THE NEW CREATURE”

Bobby the Biologist loved exploring the forest near his house...

  • Cards with organisms’ names and traits (e.g., mammal, bird, reptile, fungus).
  • A blank classification chart for students to fill in.

Instructions:

  1. Divide students into groups and provide each group with a set of organism cards.
  2. Students classify the organisms based on their traits, filling out the chart for each one.
  3. Groups share how they classified their mystery organism and justify their reasoning.

DEBRIEF DISCUSSION

  • Why is it important to classify organisms?
  • What would happen if we didn’t have a system to organize life?
  • How does classification help scientists study and protect the environment?
  • How might classification of organisms help us protect endangered species or ecosystems?
  • How does classifying organisms help us understand ecosystems such as coral reefs and forests?
