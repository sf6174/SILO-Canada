LESSON 4: NALA AND THE SNOW LEOPARDS

Nala the snow leopard, her mate Mufasa, and their cubs were thriving in their snowy mountain home. But rising temperatures caused glaciers to melt, drying up water sources. Vegetation shrank, and prey animals moved to lower altitudes.

To survive, the snow leopard family had to leave their home and face new challenges in the valleys — heat, competition, and even humans. Their journey teaches us how climate change can disrupt entire ecosystems.

THINK ABOUT IT

  • Why did Nala and her family have to leave their home?
  • What role do glaciers play in keeping mountain ecosystems healthy?

THE DOMINO EFFECT

Let’s create a climate feedback loop! Reorder these events to see how everything is connected:

  1. Rising temperatures → Glaciers melt
  2. Glaciers melt → Loss of water supply
  3. Loss of water supply → Decrease in vegetation
  4. Decrease in vegetation → Herbivores migrate
  5. Herbivores migrate → Predators like snow leopards are displaced

BIODIVERSITY RESCUE BOARD GAME

Play a game to explore how protecting biodiversity can help reverse climate change!

Objective

Each player draws an action card. If it helps the environment, move forward (lower temperature). If it harms, move backward (higher temperature). Reach the finish to bring Nala’s family back home!

Materials

  • Game board
  • Player tokens
  • Action cards

DEBRIEF

  • How does climate change affect ecosystems and biodiversity?
  • What small actions can we take to protect biodiversity?
  • How does protecting nature benefit humans?
